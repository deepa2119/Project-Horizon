# Project Horizon

> **A long-term AI-powered research operating system for learning, experimentation, automation, and knowledge management.**

---

## Vision

Project Horizon exists to solve a simple problem:

Technology evolves faster than any individual can continuously learn, organize, and connect information.

Every day, new AI models, research papers, GitHub projects, crypto protocols, DevOps tools, hardware optimizations, and engineering techniques are released. Most of this information is consumed, briefly remembered, and eventually forgotten.

Project Horizon is designed to change that.

Its purpose is to create a personal knowledge engine that captures information, preserves understanding, connects ideas across domains, and helps transform curiosity into long-term value.

The objective is not simply to store notes.

The objective is to build an AI-assisted system that grows more useful every day.

---

# Mission

Build a research platform that can:

* Capture knowledge with minimal effort.
* Organize information consistently.
* Preserve experiments and decisions.
* Connect related ideas automatically.
* Automate repetitive research tasks.
* Assist in making better technical decisions.
* Enable creation of tools, projects, and products from accumulated knowledge.

---

# Core Principles

## 1. Knowledge over Information

Saving links is not knowledge.

Every captured resource should answer at least one question:

* Why is this important?
* What did I learn?
* Where can I use it?

---

## 2. Everything is Searchable

Knowledge should never exist only inside memory.

Every experiment, decision, setup, benchmark, and idea should be searchable.

---

## 3. Every Experiment is Reproducible

Months or years later, every experiment should be understandable and reproducible from its documentation.

---

## 4. Automation Before Repetition

If a task is performed repeatedly, it should eventually be automated.

Human effort should be spent on thinking, not repetition.

---

## 5. Decisions Must Be Recorded

Remembering *why* a decision was made is often more valuable than remembering the decision itself.

Major technical decisions should be documented.

---

## 6. Simplicity Wins

The system should remain understandable after years of growth.

Complexity should only be introduced when it clearly provides long-term value.

---

## 7. Curiosity with Direction

Exploring new technologies is encouraged.

However, exploration should contribute to a growing body of knowledge rather than becoming isolated experiments.

---

# Research Domains

Project Horizon currently focuses on:

* Artificial Intelligence
* Local LLMs
* DevOps & Cloud Infrastructure
* Linux & Self-hosting
* Automation
* Hardware & Performance
* Cryptocurrencies & Blockchain
* Emerging Technologies

The architecture should remain flexible enough to support additional domains in the future.

---

# Long-Term Goal

The long-term objective is to build a personal AI research assistant capable of:

* Understanding accumulated knowledge.
* Answering questions using personal research.
* Connecting ideas across different technical domains.
* Suggesting new opportunities.
* Assisting with technical decision making.
* Supporting the development of future products and automation.

---

# Repository Philosophy

This repository is the single source of truth for Project Horizon.

Nothing important should exist only:

* in browser bookmarks,
* in Downloads,
* in temporary chats,
* or in memory.

Knowledge belongs inside Horizon.

---

# Success Criteria

Project Horizon is successful if it enables continuous learning without losing previous knowledge.

Success is measured not by the number of notes collected, but by the ability to retrieve, understand, connect, and apply accumulated knowledge over time.
